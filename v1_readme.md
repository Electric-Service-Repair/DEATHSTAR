The Death Star’s internal structure (DS-1 Orbital Battle Station) was characterized as a massive, 160-kilometer-wide, multi-layered, spherical space station designed for both functionality as a superweapon and as a self-contained habitat,,. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] 


Key aspects of the Death Star's directory/structural organization included: 

Core and Layers: The station was built with a central, hollowed-out area containing the main hypermatter reactor, which accounted for approximately 36% of the first Death Star’s diameter. The interior was structured in a layered, "onion-like" manner. 

Dual Gravity Orientation: 

Outer Shell: Areas near the surface utilized concentric decks with artificial gravity directed toward the station’s core. 

Inner Core: Deeper, central areas were designed with stacked horizontal decks, where gravity was directed towards the station's southern pole. 

Key Sectors: The layout included vast, specialized sectors: 

The Command Sector: Contained the bridge, command center, and high-ranking officer quarters. 

Technical/Engineering Sectors: Housed the superlaser components, including the 8 tributary beam towers. 

Detention Block: A specialized, high-security area for holding and interrogating prisoners. 

Industrial/Hangars: Massive areas for docking TIE fighters, shuttles, and supporting logistical operations. 

Housing/Amenities: Numerous levels dedicated to staff, featuring housing, cafeterias, and recreation areas. 

Surface Features: The exterior was dominated by the equatorial trench (a massive, orbital-level thoroughfare for logistics) and the northern hemisphere dish of the superlaser. [4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11] 

The second Death Star (DS-2) followed similar principles but was larger and had a more refined reactor structure (taking up 13% of the diameter) to allow for faster superlaser recharge times. [4, 10]
